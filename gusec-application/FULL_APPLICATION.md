# Problem Statement: The Indian Cyber Scam Crisis

## Scale of the Problem
- 1.2 million cybercrime complaints filed in India (2025)
- Rs.10,000+ crores lost annually to digital fraud
- Average Indian receives 12+ scam SMS per week
- Rural users are disproportionately targeted

## Why Global Solutions Fail in India
1. **Linguistic Gap:** Global filters don't understand Hinglish, 
   transliterated Hindi, or regional language mixing
2. **Authority Blindness:** "APDCL" (Assam Power), "PM Kisan", 
   "Ayushman Bharat" are unknown to Western-trained models
3. **Hardware Inequality:** Enterprise security requires servers. 
   90% of Indian users are on sub-Rs.15,000 devices
4. **Cloud Dependency:** Most solutions send data to US/EU servers — 
   violating DPDPA and creating latency on slow Indian networks

## The Vishing Emergency
Scammers are pivoting from URLs (which get blocked) to phone numbers. 
"Call-only" scams now represent 40%+ of Indian fraud. No existing 
open-source tool addresses this vector.

## National Security Implications
- Critical infrastructure (electricity boards, banks, government schemes) 
  are being impersonated at scale
- No indigenous, sovereign defense layer exists
- Dependence on foreign security software creates strategic vulnerability
